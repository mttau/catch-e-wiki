Overview

Billing schedules are constructed when the relevant fields are entered in Contracts / Billing and saved. "Periods Deferred" (via the quote), "Initial Billings", "Start Period" and "Common Day" all default in from Clients / Contract Defaults. These can be set-up to provide a default billing schedule to suit your requirements, but can be edited within each contract.
Below are examples of various outcomes you can arrange.

[edit]

Pay Cycle Billing

The billing structure created when a pay cycle is used is a linear schedule based on the "Periodic Interval" and the "Start Periodic". Users must select the "Start Periodic" for each contract as it is built.

NOTE: If the "Start Periodic" field is left blank, than it will not be included in the billing run. This includes scheduled billing jobs.

[edit]

Monthly Billing

Monthly billing can be organised in various way - the following business rules and examples will help illustrate what can be done.

  • The date entered in "Start Periodic" is always the date used to create Period 1 of the schedule.
  • "Start Periodic" must be on or after the "Contract Start".
  • The day entered in "Common Day" is always the day used to build the billing date for Period 2 onwards (this does not have to match the "Start Periodic" day).
  • If "Start Period" = 1 and the constructed Period 2 Date is greater than "Contract Start", Period 2 will fall in the same calendar month as Period 1, this is also referred to as "double billing". (E.g. C and D)
  • If "Start Period" = 2+, Period 2 will be dated in the next month on from Period 1 (E.g. E and F).

[edit]

Vary Billing

You can use a combination of "Next Period", "Common Day" and "Next Periodic" to achieve the same outcomes for modified contracts. In particular, if you set "Next Period" = 1, you can create a double billing, like example C above.
Note ~ the "Next Periodic" will still save as required, but changing the value to '1' enables the double billing business rule to work.

[edit]
