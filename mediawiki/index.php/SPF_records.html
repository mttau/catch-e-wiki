Understanding SPF records

We recommend that you create a Sender Policy Framework (SPF) record for your domain. An SPF record is a type of Domain Name Service (DNS) record that identifies which mail servers are permitted to send email on behalf of your domain.

The purpose of an SPF record is to prevent spammers from sending messages with false "From" addresses at your domain.

If your domain does not have an SPF record for Catch-e, emails sent from the system on your behalf may be identified as spam and rejected, as the sender domain (catch-e.com.au) and the "From" (user@client.com.au) are not the same domain name (see also the Mail Failed page).

This web page has a good detailed explanation; MailChimp SPF Information.

[edit]

To create an SPF record for your domain

  • Log in to the administrative console for your domain.
  • Locate the page from which you can update the DNS records.
    • You may need to enable advanced settings.
  • Create a TXT record containing this text: v=spf1 include:_spf.catch-e.com.au ~all
    • Publishing an SPF record that uses -all instead of ~all may result in delivery problems.
  • Save your changes.
    • Keep in mind that changes to DNS records may take up to 48 hours to propagate throughout the Internet.

If you want to specify the raw SPF data in your zone record rather than using the include: syntax you can obtain the TXT data by issuing a dig request 

# dig -4 TXT _spf.catch-e.com.au

Keep in mind, that maintaining the accuracy of the SPF record will become your responsibility. If you would like Catch-e to notify you of future SPF changes, please let us know.

Refer also to the Domain Setup page, you will need to confirm with Catch-e when your SPF Record has been created so the registration process of your Domain can be completed.If you have difficulty creating an SPF record, contact your domain provider or your Catch-e Account Manager for further assistance.

[edit]

To check an SPF record for your domain

There are various websites where you can check the SPF status of your domain.

kitterman is a good example which provides a SPF Policy Tester

Simply enter a Catch-e IP address used for outbound email and a sample email address that incorporates your domain name.

For an IP address use either:

52.64.49.97 or 52.64.24.222

In the sender address field, you would type:

contact@[clientdomainname].com.au
