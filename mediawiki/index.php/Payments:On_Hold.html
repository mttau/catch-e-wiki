[edit]

On Hold field behaviour

  • The "On Hold" check box and date fields are only editable when the button has been clicked.
  • The user must first check ON the check box to enable the date field.
  • Once checked, the date field is populated with today's date.
  • While checked, the date field is editable and a date selector is available.
  • The “On Hold” check box and “On Hold” date fields always act in unison, i.e. if checked ON, a date will be populated, if checked OFF, the date will be removed.
  • The On Hold function is expected to be mainly used in automated updates, but if used on screen, it’s expected use is for putting pending transactions on hold due to credit issues.
  • If the payment record contains any rows in 'On Hold’ status, those rows are displayed with red text and a pink background (similar to ‘Suspended’ contracts).


[edit]

On Hold and Stop Payment relationship

  • The “Stop Payment” check box and the “On Hold” check box are mutually exclusive.
  • Records can either be stopped or on hold, not both.
  • If “Stop Payments” is checked and the “On Hold” check box is already on, the “On Hold” check and date are removed. This also works in reverse.
  • A number of processes populate the “Payment Stop” checkbox, date and update schedule rows, these also remove the “On Hold” checkbox, date and row statuses if used.

Stop processes:-

  • If "Stop Payment" is not already ticked and dated, the following processes will populate the checkbox and date fields:
  • If a vehicle is suspended - check box ON and "Stop Payment" date is updated with the suspended date.
  • If a vehicle is terminated without going through a suspend process - check box ON, update "Stop Payment" date with the terminated date.
  • If a vehicle is paid out or sold - check box ON, update "Stop Payment" date with the paid-out date or sold date. Apply whichever date is earliest.


[edit]

Impact on Payment Schedules after adding an On Hold record

  • If an On Hold check/date is performed, all "Pending" transactions in the Periodic Payables schedules that are dated to occur on or after the On Hold date have their status changed to ‘On Hold’.
  • If an On Hold check/date is changed, all ‘Pending’ and ‘On Hold’ transactions are re-cast according to the new On Hold date.
  • Payment rows with a Due Date that is before the On Hold date are updated to ‘Pending’
  • Payment Rows with a Due Date that is on or after the On Hold date are changed to ‘On Hold’
  • If an On Hold check /date is removed (un-checked and date blanked out) - all ‘On Hold’ transactions are to be re-cast as ‘Pending’, unless the removal was forced by the “Stop Payment” being checked, in which case, the transactions will be re-cast as ‘pending’ or ‘stopped’.
  • If a On Hold check date is added and it occurs after the last scheduled payment date (i.e. it does not lead to a record selection, the check and date are to be discarded on Save).
