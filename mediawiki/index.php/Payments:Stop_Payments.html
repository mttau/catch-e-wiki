Overview

Checking and dating the "Stop Payments" fields will set all the 'Pending' rows in the schedule with a "Due Date" that is on or after this date to 'Stopped'.
'Stopped' payment records are no longer available for payment selection.
If a payment is stopped, the "On Hold" function is turned off and the payment rows are re-set to either 'Pending' or 'Stopped', depending on their due dates.

[edit]

Stop Payments

  • The "Stop Payments" check box and date are blank until you populate them, or they are updated from another screen.
  • The check box and date fields are available for editing when you click the Vary button.
  • When you first check the "Stop Payments" check box, the date field is populated with today's date.
  • You can edit the date by over typing a new date or by using the date selector .
  • The "Stop Payments" check box and date always act in unison, i.e. if checked, a date will be added, if un-checked, the date will be removed
[edit]

Stop Payments updated by other processes

If "Stop Payments" is not already checked and dated, performing the following data updates will lead to the "Stop Payments" check box and date being populated for all of the contract's payment schedules.
The contract's "Suspend Date" is used to update the "Stop Payments" date:

  • The Contracts / Contract "Suspend Date" or "Terminated" date is populated.
  • The Contracts / Order "Contract Status" is updated to "Suspended" or "Terminated".
  • The Contracts / Terminate "Contract Status" is updated to "Suspended" or "Terminated".
  • The Approvals / Contract "Suspend Date" or "Terminated" date is populated.

Note:

  • Removing the "Suspend Date" in any of the screens listed above does not reverse the stop that was performed.
  • To reverse a stop, you must go to Contracts / Payments and un-check "Stop Payments" directly.
[edit]

When Stop Payments is added

When "Stop Payments" is checked and dated;

  • All the rows in the schedule with a "Due Date" that fall before this date remain at (or are updated to) 'Pending'.
  • All the rows in the schedule with a "Due Date" that fall or after this date are updated to 'Stopped'.
  • If the payment is "On Hold", the "On Hold" checkbox is un-checked and the date removed, all payment rows that were 'On Hold', will become either 'Pending' or 'Stopped'.
  • If no 'Pending' records remain at this point, the payment schedule will be updated to 'Inactive' status.
[edit]

When Stop Payments is removed

When "Stop Payments" is un-checked;

  • The "Stop Payments" date is removed.
  • All 'Stopped' records are returned to 'Pending'
  • If 'Pending' records exist at this point, the payment schedule will be updated to 'Active' status.
