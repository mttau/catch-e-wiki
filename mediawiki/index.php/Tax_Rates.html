Overview

The Australian Government has legislated Income Tax Rates and PAYG Tax which are regularly reviewed and updated as part of the Annual Budget submission. What this means, is when an individualâ€™s income exceeds a set threshold, specific income tax rates apply. Catch-e updates Income Tax Rates and PAYG Tax in accordance with the passed legislation, so they will be used in quote calculations from the set date.

PAYG Tax

PAYG withholding tax is also part of the Tax Calculations and are also set up in Catch-e to be included in the quoting process, where applicable.
They are stored in the Global Controls table as 'payg_coefficients_scale_1' to 'payg_coefficients_scale_7'.
For more information, please visit the ATO website via http://softwaredevelopers.ato.gov.au/PAYGWTaxtables

Statutory or benchmark interest rate

Benchmark rates are sometimes used in fringe benefit calculations. Benchmark rates are published by the Australian Taxation Office on each change as a 'Taxation Determination'

Catch-e stores the benchmark rate that is used to calculate Deemed Interest. It is stored as [gb_controls]ato_benchmark_interest.

It is used to calculate the taxable value of:

  1. a fringe benefit provided by way of a loan, and
  2. a car fringe benefit where an employer chooses to value the benefit using 'Operating cost method'.
